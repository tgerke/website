Needing a site redesign, I spent several weeks fiddling with skeleton and, later, Foundation off and on. There just weren’t enough hours in the day. Then, I spotted the following tweet from Julia Silge, and just over a week later, all is good using blogdown. Who would have thought web design could be done mostly in R?

I won’t give a thorough walkthrough; thankfully, this has been beautifully covered by Amber Thomas here, and the authors of blogdown are currently working on a web-book here. Here are the few lines that built this thing though:

library(devtools)
devtools::install_github("rstudio/blogdown")
blogdown::install_hugo()

setwd("/myWebsiteDirectory/")

blogdown::new_site(theme = "nurlansu/hugo-sustain")

I later decided I also wanted to add in a CV page with the Vitae format, so I created a subdirectory for those pages:

setwd("/myWebsiteDirectory/vitae")

blogdown::new_site(theme = "nurlansu/hugo-vitae")
